Scholarhips for Recreation and Leisure Studies majors.

Unless otherwise noted, application deadline for scholarships is January 31
Name Award Status Criteria
Keene Spirit of Service Award $1,000 total/$500 per semester Full-time Undergraduate or Graduate
  • RCLS Major
  • 3.0 GPA
  • Significant service and leadership to the community
  • Financial need
  • Interview
Charles A. Vincent Memorial Scholarship $1,000 total/$500 per semester Full-time Undergraduate
  • RCLS major
  • Two letters of recommendation from educators
  • Two letters of recommendation from professional practitioners
  • Resume
  • Interview
  • 3.00 overall GPA
  • 3.00 GPA in RCLS courses
Michael Bunting Scholarship $1,200 per year total/$600 per semester Full-time Undergraduate
  • RCLS major
  • 3.0 GPA
  • Document financial need, academic merit, leadership potential, dedication to area of study
  • Must be NC resident
Michael Duquette Scholarship $1,000 total/$500 per semester Full-time Undergraduate or Graduate
  • Students who demonstrate scholastic abilities in biofeedback and recreational therapy, are caring and compassionate
  • Must have completed biofeedback courses, or currently enrolled
  • Cumulative GPA of 3.5 or greater
  • Submit 1 page paper, single spaced, on the importance of empathy, biofeedback, and recreation in the treatment process
Peggy Pruett Cromer Scholarship $1,000 total/$500 per semester Full-time Undergraduate
  • Demonstrate educational leadership potential
  • Involvement in profession
  • 2.5 GPA
  • Recreational Therapy Major
Joseph and Hannah Tart Scholarship Warrior Training Program $1,500 total/$750 per semester Graduate
  • Cover letter
  • Working in the Center for Applied Psychophysiology (Biofeedback Program)
  • 3.0 GPA
Robert L. Jones Scholarship for the Wounded Warriors Training Program $1,500 total/$750 per semester Full-time Undergraduate or Graduate
  • 3.0 GPA
  • Cover Letter
  • One letter of recommendation
Two types of students are eligible:
  1. Enrolled wounded warriors who are participating in the ECU wounded warriors training program; (first priority)
  2. Students who will be working with ECU wounded warrior training program during the time the award is given (secondary priority)
John & Joan Taylor Scholarship $1,000 total/$500 per semester Full time Undergraduate
  • RCLS major
  • 3.0 GPA
  • Financial need
  • Involvement in student leadership activities (departmental, college or university level)
Michael L. Bunting Scholarship for Warrior Training $1,000 total/$500 per semester Graduate
  • RCLS major
  • Working in the Center for Applied Psychophysiology (Biofeedback Program)
  • Must have completed biofeedback course(s) or currently enrolled
  • NC resident
